I'm a Development Economist from Rome, driven by a deep curiosity about how societies grow, organize, and adapt. My current research focuses mainly on the economics of crime and conflicts, but I also touched topics as public policy and policy evaluation, industrial economics, and I work in labour economics at the National Council of Economics and Labour.

I am also an outdoors and adventure enthusiast — from mountaineering to sailing, and a former competitive fencer and sailor who reached continental-level competitions. I have long been actively involved in youth education through scouting, where I now serve as a provincial coordinator.
